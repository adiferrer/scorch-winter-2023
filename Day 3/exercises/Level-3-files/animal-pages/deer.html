Deer are a group of hoofed mammals that are found throughout the world in a variety of habitats, including forests, grasslands, and deserts. There are many species of deer, including the white-tailed deer, mule deer, elk, caribou, and moose.

Deer are herbivores, feeding on a variety of plant material, including leaves, twigs, and grasses. They are important prey animals for many predators, including wolves, coyotes, and mountain lions.

Deer have long legs that allow them to run quickly and jump over obstacles. They also have antlers, which are used by male deer to establish dominance and attract mates. Female deer, called does, do not have antlers.

Deer are known for their seasonal migrations, which can cover hundreds of miles as they move between summer and winter ranges. They also exhibit a variety of social behaviors, including grooming, vocalizations, and posturing.

Deer populations are managed in many areas to maintain a healthy balance between the deer population and the ecosystem. This includes hunting and culling programs, as well as habitat conservation efforts. Deer are also popular animals for recreational hunting and wildlife watching, contributing to their economic value.
